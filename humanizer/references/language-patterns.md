# Language and grammar patterns (7-13)

These are word-level and sentence-shape tells. Even after content is fixed, sentences can still read AI because of vocabulary choice, copula avoidance, and rhetorical scaffolding ("not just X, but Y", rule-of-three, false ranges, passive voice).

---

## 7. Overused "AI vocabulary" words

**High-frequency AI words:** actually, additionally, align with, crucial, delve, emphasizing, enduring, enhance, fostering, garner, highlight (verb), interplay, intricate, intricacies, key (adjective), landscape (abstract), pivotal, showcase, tapestry (abstract), testament, underscore (verb), valuable, vibrant.

**Why it reads as AI:** These words appear far more frequently in post-2023 text than in human writing. They cluster — once you see one, the next two are often nearby.

**Before:**
> Additionally, a distinctive feature of Somali cuisine is the incorporation of camel meat. An enduring testament to Italian colonial influence is the widespread adoption of pasta in the local culinary landscape, showcasing how these dishes have integrated into the traditional diet.

**After:**
> Somali cuisine also includes camel meat, which is considered a delicacy. Pasta dishes, introduced during Italian colonization, remain common, especially in the south.

**Fix recipe:** Treat the list as a blacklist. When you see one, find a plain alternative or delete the clause entirely. "Delve into X" → "look at X" or just "X". "Showcase" → "show". "Underscore" → cut, the sentence usually still works.

---

## 8. Avoidance of "is" / "are" (copula avoidance)

**Words to watch:** serves as, stands as, marks, represents, boasts, features, offers (when followed by "a [thing]").

**Why it reads as AI:** LLMs substitute elaborate constructions for simple copulas. Real writing uses is/are/has by default and reaches for "serves as" only when the metaphor is load-bearing.

**Before:**
> Gallery 825 serves as LAAA's exhibition space for contemporary art. The gallery features four separate spaces and boasts over 3,000 square feet.

**After:**
> Gallery 825 is LAAA's exhibition space for contemporary art. The gallery has four rooms totaling 3,000 square feet.

**Fix recipe:** Direct swap. "X serves as Y" → "X is Y". "X boasts/features Y" → "X has Y". "X stands as Y" → "X is Y".

---

## 9. Negative parallelisms and tailing negations

**Why it reads as AI:** Constructions like "not just X, but Y" or "It's not merely X, it's Y" are overused for fake gravitas. Tailing-negation fragments — "no guessing", "no wasted motion" — appended to a sentence sound like sales copy.

**Before:**
> It's not just about the beat riding under the vocals; it's part of the aggression and atmosphere. It's not merely a song, it's a statement.

**After:**
> The heavy beat adds to the aggressive tone.

**Before (tailing negation):**
> The options come from the selected item, no guessing.

**After:**
> The options come from the selected item without forcing the user to guess.

**Fix recipe:** "Not just X, but Y" → just say Y. The X half is usually filler. For tailing negations, expand them into a real clause with a subject and verb.

---

## 10. Rule of three overuse

**Why it reads as AI:** LLMs force ideas into groups of three to feel comprehensive. Real writing uses one item, two items, or four — rarely a perfect rhythmic triplet on every sentence.

**Before:**
> The event features keynote sessions, panel discussions, and networking opportunities. Attendees can expect innovation, inspiration, and industry insights.

**After:**
> The event includes talks and panels. There's also time for informal networking between sessions.

**Fix recipe:** Count the triplets in a paragraph. If there's more than one, kill at least one. Replace with one item, two, or a longer clause that gives detail on a single thing.

---

## 11. Elegant variation (synonym cycling)

**Why it reads as AI:** AI has repetition-penalty code that causes excessive synonym substitution. The result reads like a thesaurus in motion.

**Before:**
> The protagonist faces many challenges. The main character must overcome obstacles. The central figure eventually triumphs. The hero returns home.

**After:**
> The protagonist faces many challenges but eventually triumphs and returns home.

**Fix recipe:** Pick one term and stick with it. Pronouns are also fine — "he", "she", "they", "it" beat the third synonym swap.

---

## 12. False ranges

**Why it reads as AI:** LLMs use "from X to Y" constructions where X and Y are not on a meaningful scale. The construction sounds comprehensive but covers nothing definable.

**Before:**
> Our journey through the universe has taken us from the singularity of the Big Bang to the grand cosmic web, from the birth and death of stars to the enigmatic dance of dark matter.

**After:**
> The book covers the Big Bang, star formation, and current theories about dark matter.

**Fix recipe:** If X and Y aren't endpoints of a real scale (small to large, early to late, simple to complex), the "from X to Y" is fake. Replace with a flat list.

---

## 13. Passive voice and subjectless fragments

**Why it reads as AI:** LLMs hide the actor or drop the subject entirely with lines like "No configuration file needed" or "The results are preserved automatically." Active voice with a real subject is clearer and more direct.

**Before:**
> No configuration file needed. The results are preserved automatically.

**After:**
> You do not need a configuration file. The system preserves the results automatically.

**Fix recipe:** Find the actor. Make the actor the subject of the sentence. If the actor is genuinely unknown or irrelevant (which is rare), passive is fine — but most AI passive is hiding nothing, just being lazy.
