# Business / product pattern

Use this pattern when evaluating a business strategy, product idea, market entry plan, go-to-market motion, pricing model, or startup concept. Deliverable shape: revised go-to-market plan, realistic timeline with milestones, top 3 existential risks with mitigations, honest "is this worth pursuing" assessment.

## Foundation (Q1-Q10)

- What is the goal, and how do we get there?
- Who is the customer and what pain are we solving?
- Why now? What changed?
- What is the business model?
- What is the market size?
- Who are the existing players?
- What is the unfair advantage?
- What does the MVP look like?
- What is the timeline to revenue?
- What resources are available?

## Mechanics (Q11-Q30)

- Customer acquisition strategy
- Pricing model and packaging
- Cost structure (CAC, COGS, support, ops)
- Key partnerships needed
- Technical requirements
- Regulatory considerations
- Go-to-market plan and channels
- Success metrics and KPIs
- Team structure needed
- Funding requirements
- Sales cycle length and complexity
- Onboarding and time-to-first-value
- Retention and expansion mechanics
- Support model (self-serve, assisted, white-glove)
- Brand positioning and messaging
- Distribution channel economics
- Cash collection vs revenue recognition
- Unit economics at scale
- Capital efficiency
- International / localization scope (if applicable)

## Stress testing (Q31-Q50)

- What if a large competitor enters?
- What if customer acquisition costs double?
- What if the market shrinks?
- What if key team members leave?
- What if funding falls through?
- What if the regulatory environment changes?
- What is the worst-case scenario and can we survive it?
- What if the underlying platform we depend on changes terms?
- What if the macro environment turns hostile?
- What if a free or open-source alternative emerges?
- What if customer churn doubles?
- What if our biggest customer leaves?
- What if a key supplier raises prices?
- What if pricing pressure forces a 50% cut?
- What if we miss the funding window?
- What if a critical compliance requirement lands mid-year?

## Competitive / alternative analysis (Q51-Q65)

- What existing products, services, or workflows already address this pain?
- Why would customers switch from the default alternative?
- What would a well-funded competitor copy first?
- What advantage is hard to copy?
- What distribution channel could become a bottleneck?
- What pricing, packaging, or positioning assumptions are unproven?
- What would make this idea irrelevant in 12 months?
- What's the "do nothing" alternative for the customer?
- What's the build-it-yourself alternative for the customer?
- What incumbents are likely to bundle this into their existing product?

## Feasibility (Q66-Q80)

- Can the founding team actually execute this?
- What is the minimum viable team?
- What needs to be true for this to work?
- What is the path to first 10 customers? First 100?
- What partnerships are required vs nice-to-have?
- What is the burn rate and runway?
- What regulatory approvals are needed?
- What does the 12-month operating plan look like?
- What's the path to break-even?
- What is the exit story?

## Refinement (Q81-Q90)

- What features can be cut from the MVP?
- What is the simplest possible version that validates the hypothesis?
- What would a customer pay for today, not someday?
- What operational complexity can be eliminated?
- What pricing change would unblock the most customers?
- What positioning change would clarify "why us"?
- What can be sequenced later without losing the wedge?

## Synthesis (Q91-Q100)

- Revised go-to-market plan
- Realistic timeline with milestones
- Top 3 existential risks and mitigations
- Funding plan and milestones tied to it
- Hiring plan tied to milestones
- Pricing and packaging recommendation
- Honest assessment: is this worth pursuing?
